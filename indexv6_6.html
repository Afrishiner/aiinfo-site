AI to Eye — Issue #1

A light, human read for decision makers — curated insights, quick takeaways, and links to dive deeper.

1) Headline & one-line takeaway

Gemini and Copilot push deeper into everyday tools. Expect faster rollout pressure across your stack — pick narrow, high-ROI use cases first.

2) Why it matters for operators

Vendor-led AI features now ship by default (browsers, office suites, home/IoT). You’ll see usage whether you plan for it or not. Formalise guidance, data boundaries, and measurement.

3) Quick wins this week

  • Ship a one-page AI usage policy addendum for staff.
  • Create a shared “AI Wins” board to capture ideas and outcomes.
  • Identify 2 repetitive workflows for pilot automation (e.g., inbox triage, client notes → CRM).

4) Risks & gotchas

  • Default-on deployments (e.g., office apps) can create shadow processes. Brief your helpdesk.
  • Beware over-collection in logs and prompts; turn on data controls.
  • Track quality drift — new model versions can subtly change outputs.

5) Case snippet

Mid-market services firm cut proposal drafting time 35% by templating prompts + auto-assembling packages from a vetted content library. No model change, only workflow change.

6) Tool of the week

Gemini in Chrome — quick page summaries and task automations directly in the browser.

7) Prompt pattern

You are my <role>. Our goal: <goal>.
Context you must use: <bullet points>.
Constraints: <limits, tone, format>.
Produce: <exact artifact> with <sections>.
Then: suggest 3 refinements.

8) Read / watch

  • Reuters: Google adds Gemini to Chrome
  • The Verge: New Nest cams built for Gemini
  • TechRadar: Microsoft 365 Copilot auto-installs in Oct

9) What we’re testing

Light-touch browser agents for research ops; measuring impact vs. curated reading lists.
